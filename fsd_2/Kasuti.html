Kasuti

Kasuti is a domestic fine folk art that commonly depicted subjects that women witnessed in their everyday surroundings and essentials.

Their environment had animals and birds like elephants, deer, swans and peacocks. We also see Kasuti depict magnificent and elaborate patterns of the religious structures that were built in that time like the Gopura (temple), Deepa (candle), Tulsi Katte, Teru (chariot) and more. Kasuti was the medium to symbolise Karnataka, the women of that time, the traditions, customs, and lifestyle of their people. The motifs were recorded over centuries that have now become the framework to the Kasuti art form Badami, Karnataka. The empire played a vital role in the revival of Art & Culture A Golden Age for the South. We created our collection with the women of Sakhi Saphalya in Dharwad. A community of artisans that are all aiming to revive dying crafts and preserve the rare art forms of North Karnataka created centuries ago, go back to our roots, and relive the stories of our ancestors.
